Genetic Diseases and Conditions

Disease Recommendations Causes and Effects Percentage
Down Syndrome Trisomy of chromosome 21 leading to developmental delays, intellectual disability, and distinctive physical features. Early intervention programs, medical monitoring for associated health issues, and supportive care. Approximately 1 in 700 births.
Sickle Cell Anemia Regular medical check-ups, hydration, pain management, blood transfusions, and hydroxyurea therapy. Genetic mutation affecting hemoglobin, leading to misshapen red blood cells causing pain, anemia, and organ damage. Approximately 1 in 500 African-American births.
Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy Mutation in the dystrophin gene resulting in progressive muscle weakness and wasting. Physical therapy, corticosteroids, respiratory support, and gene therapy research. Approximately 1 in 5,000 male births..
Marfan Syndrome Genetic mutation affecting connective tissue, leading to tall stature, joint hypermobility, and cardiovascular complications. Regular cardiac monitoring, lifestyle modifications to reduce strain on the heart, and surgical interventions if necessary. Approximately 1 in 5,000 births.
